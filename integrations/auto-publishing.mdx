## Overview

Auto-publishing lets Pierview generate SEO-optimized articles on a recurring schedule and push them directly to your connected CMS. Once set up, content creation and publishing happen automatically, no manual steps required.

<Info>
  Auto-publishing requires at least one CMS integration to be connected. Set up [Framer](/integrations/framer-integration), [Sanity](/integrations/sanity-integration), or [Webflow](/integrations/webflow-integration) first.
</Info>

## How It Works

1. You choose which days of the week content should be generated.
2. On each scheduled day, Pierview generates a new article optimized for your brand and tracked keywords.
3. The article is automatically published to your connected CMS as a draft.
4. You review and publish it from your CMS when ready.

## Setting Up Auto-Publishing

1. In Pierview, go to **Content Creation**. You'll see an **Auto-Publish** box to the right of the content creation area. You can also visit this page directly from the sidebar.

2. Toggle auto-publishing **on**.

3. Select the days of the week you want content generated. We recommend **once or twice a week** to maintain a consistent publishing cadence without overwhelming your review queue.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/framer/pierview-schedule.png" alt="Auto-publish schedule settings" />
</Frame>

4. Make sure at least one CMS integration is connected and enabled. Content will be sent to all enabled integrations (Framer, Sanity, and/or Webflow) on each scheduled day.

## Publishing On Demand

You can also publish individual articles at any time without waiting for the schedule. Open any generated article and click **Publish** in the top-right sidebar to send it to your connected CMS immediately.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Content is being generated but not published">
    Check that your CMS integration is connected and the toggle is enabled. Go to [Settings → Integrations → CMS Integrations](https://pierview.ai/dashboard/configuration/integrations) to verify the connection status.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No content is being generated on scheduled days">
    Make sure auto-publishing is toggled on and at least one day of the week is selected. If the schedule looks correct, check that your account has remaining content generation credits.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
